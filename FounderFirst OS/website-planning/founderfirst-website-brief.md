# FounderFirst — Website Design Brief
*For handoff to design. Last updated: April 2026.*

---

## 1. Project Context

**FounderFirst** is a platform that builds the back-office infrastructure founders wish existed — starting with **Penny**, an AI-first bookkeeper for US sole proprietors.

This brief covers the **marketing website** (not the app). Its job is one thing: get the right founder to feel seen — and take the next step.

---

## 2. Audience

Founders, solopreneurs, and small business owners — especially solo service providers (freelancers, consultants, coaches, designers). They are capable and driven. They know their craft. They did not start a business to do admin.

Speak to them as intelligent adults who have made a brave decision. Never talk down. Never make them feel like they did something wrong.

---

## 3. Tone Guide

These principles come directly from Penny's voice guide and apply to all copy on this site.

- **Plain English always.** No accounting jargon. No startup buzzwords. If a non-industry person wouldn't understand it, rewrite it.
- **Human over corporate.** This site should sound like it was written by someone who genuinely gets what it's like to run a business alone.
- **Lead with the feeling, then the fact.** "You're drowning in admin" before "Penny handles your bookkeeping."
- **Short sentences.** One idea at a time.
- **Warm, not salesy.** We are not selling software. We are saying: someone is in your corner.
- **Celebrate what's brave.** Starting a business is hard. Acknowledge that.

---

## 4. Design Principles

Pulled from the Penny design system — apply to the website.

- **Generous whitespace.** Let copy breathe. Do not crowd.
- **No jargon on screen.** Ever.
- **Mobile-first.** Design for 375px, scale up.
- **Greyscale wireframe.** Use the Penny color tokens for reference. No color until high-fidelity.
- **Typography:** `-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'SF Pro Text', 'Segoe UI', sans-serif`
- **One call to action per section.** Not two. One.

### Color Tokens (for reference in hi-fi)

| Token | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| `ink` | `#111` | Primary text |
| `ink-secondary` | `#333` | Supporting text |
| `ink-tertiary` | `#888` | Subtitles, metadata |
| `fill-primary` | `#222` | Buttons, active states |
| `fill-card` | `#FAFAFA` | Card backgrounds |
| `fill-input` | `#F4F4F4` | Input fields |
| `border` | `#E0E0E0` | Dividers |

## 5. Page Structure & Copy

---

### SECTION 1 — Hero

**Purpose:** Stop the right founder in their tracks. Make them feel seen in the first 3 seconds.

**Layout:** Full-width. Centered. Clean. One headline, one subline, one CTA. No clutter. Light background — white or near-white.

---

**Headline:**
> You started a business to build something.
> Not to drown in admin.

**Subline:**
> FounderFirst builds the tools that quietly handle everything else — so you can stay focused on the work only you can do.

**CTA Button:**
> Meet Penny →

---

### SECTION 2 — The Problem

**Purpose:** Name the pain so founders feel genuinely understood. This is not a feature list. This is empathy.

**Layout:** Two-column on desktop. Left: an honest visual of a founder at their desk late at night — not a stock photo smile, a real moment. Right: short, punchy copy in flowing prose. On mobile: stacked, image first.

---

**Copy:**

Starting a business is one of the bravest things a person can do. You bet on yourself. You gave up the safety net. And every single day, you're figuring out things no one taught you in school.

But here's the thing nobody warns you about — the bigger your ambition, the more the *business of running a business* gets in the way.

Suddenly you're not just a founder. You're your own bookkeeper, your own finance team, your own tax advisor — usually at 11pm, when you're already exhausted.

And these aren't people who are failing. They're often doing really well. They just have no visibility. No time. And no one in their corner helping them make sense of it.

---

### SECTION 3 — FounderFirst Mission

**Purpose:** Introduce FounderFirst as the answer to Section 2. This is the "we get it, and we built something" moment.

**Layout:** Full-width. Slightly off-white or light warm background to create contrast from Section 2. Centered text. Beneath the copy — three simple icon cards in a row (or stacked on mobile).

---

**Headline:**
> That's exactly why we built FounderFirst.

**Body:**
> Your time and energy are your most valuable assets. They should go toward building what only you can build.
>
> FounderFirst is the operating layer for ambitious founders — tools and services that quietly handle the complexity, so you can stay in your zone and move faster with confidence.
>
> Think of us as the team behind your team.

**Three icon cards (outcomes, not features):**

| Icon | Label | One-line |
|---|---|---|
| 🎯 | Stay Focused | Stop switching hats. Build what you're best at. |
| ⚡ | Move Faster | Make decisions quickly when you know your numbers. |
| 😌 | Build with Confidence | No more guessing. No more end-of-year panic. |

---

### SECTION 4 — Introducing Penny

**Purpose:** Introduce the first product — warm, specific, human. Not a feature dump.

**Layout:** Two-column. Left: phone mockup / product screenshot placeholder. Right: headline + three outcome statements + CTA. On mobile: stacked, copy first.

---

**Eyebrow label (small, uppercase):**
> STARTING WITH THE BIGGEST PAIN POINT

**Headline:**
> Meet Penny.
> Your business's bookkeeper.

**Body:**
> Penny is like having a bookkeeper who actually gets your business — one who keeps your finances organized, tells you where your money is going, and makes sure you're never caught off guard at tax time.
>
> You don't need to learn accounting. You don't need to hire someone expensive. You just need to know your numbers, stay clean, and get back to the work you actually love.

**Three outcome statements (not bullet points — short bold lines followed by a sentence):**

**Always know your profit.**
Not just revenue — real profit. What you actually took home after expenses, updated as you go.

**Tax season without the panic.**
Your books are clean and organized the way your CPA needs them. No shoebox. No scramble.

**Someone always in your corner.**
Penny flags what matters, handles what's routine, and asks only when she genuinely needs you.

**CTA Button:**
> Get Early Access →

## 6. Wireframe Design Prompt

Use the following prompt with Figma AI, v0.dev, Framer AI, or any wireframe tool:

---

> Design a clean, modern, single-page marketing website wireframe for a startup called **FounderFirst** and its first product **Penny** — an AI bookkeeper for solo business owners.
>
> **Tone:** Warm, empathetic, human. Not corporate. Not SaaS-generic. This should feel like a trusted advisor wrote it, not a growth marketer.
>
> **Design principles:**
> - Mobile-first. Design for 375px, scale to desktop.
> - Greyscale wireframe only. No color decisions yet.
> - Generous whitespace. Let copy breathe.
> - Sans-serif font stack: SF Pro Text / Segoe UI. Three type sizes only: hero (36px), body (15px), label (11px uppercase).
> - One CTA per section. Never two.
> - No stock photo energy. Visuals should feel real and grounded.
>
> **Page sections (in order):**
>
> 1. **Hero** — Full-width centered. Bold two-line headline. One-line subline. Single CTA button. Clean background.
>
> 2. **The Problem** — Two-column (image left, copy right). Honest, late-night-founder visual on left. Emotional flowing copy on right naming the pain: bookkeeping, tax panic, no visibility. Three pull-quote lines in a different visual treatment.
>
> 3. **FounderFirst Mission** — Full-width, slightly contrasting background. Centered headline and body. Three icon cards beneath (Focus / Speed / Confidence).
>
> 4. **Meet Penny** — Two-column. Phone product mockup left. Headline + three outcome statements + CTA right. Eyebrow label above headline.
>
> 5. **Founder Voices** — Full-width band. Warm background. Three clearly labelled placeholder quote blocks (`[FOUNDER QUOTE 1]` etc.) with placeholder avatar circles and name/role. One factual intro line above. Do not fill in example quotes — these will be real founder interviews.
>
> 6. **Closing CTA** — Centered. Large headline. Email capture field + button. Small reassurance note below.
>
> 7. **Footer** — Minimal. Logo left. Tagline. Two–three nav links. Legal line.
>
> **Color tokens (for reference — apply in hi-fi only):**
> - Primary text: `#111`
> - Buttons / active: `#222`
> - Cards: `#FAFAFA`
> - Borders: `#E0E0E0`
> - Supporting text: `#888`
