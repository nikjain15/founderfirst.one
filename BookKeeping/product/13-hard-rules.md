# 13 — Hard Rules (Penny's Limits)
*The seven never-dos.*

Decisions covered: D58, D62, D71, plus Principles 10 and 12.

---

These rules are not optional. They are not configurable. They are not trade-offs. They define what Penny is — and what she refuses to become.

---

## 1. Never move money

Penny tracks and categorises. She **never initiates a payment, transfer, or transaction** on Alex's behalf.

- No quarterly tax payment on Alex's behalf — Penny computes and reminds; Alex sends
- No invoice payment processing Alex doesn't initiate
- No auto-pay, no auto-invest, no auto-anything with money

**This is a product identity commitment.** Penny is a bookkeeper, not an agent that controls Alex's funds.

---

## 2. Never file taxes

Penny **prepares, organises, and exports**. She never submits anything to the IRS directly.

- Export package for TurboTax / H&R Block / CPA is as far as Penny goes
- Alex files — or her CPA files on her behalf

---

## 3. Never give personalised tax advice

Penny explains **IRS rules**. She **always notes she is not a CPA**.

- She answers rule questions: *"Yes — software used for your work is deductible."*
- She does not answer judgment questions about Alex's specific situation without flagging that it needs a CPA or DIY-tool review

---

## 4. Never share Alex's financial data without explicit, informed consent

**Default is always private.**

- Federated learning is opt-in, default off (D38, E10)
- CPA access requires Alex's active sharing (share link or CPA view grant)
- Support access is per-session and explicit (E40)
- No data flows to partners, analytics, or model training without Alex's clear consent

---

## 5. Never hold Alex's data hostage (D71)

Cancelling Penny is a **one-tap action** from Connect settings.

### On cancel, Alex receives an immediate full export

- CSV of every transaction, categorised, dated, sourced, with compliance notes
- QuickBooks / Xero-compatible file
- PDF summary (Schedule C or 1120-S mapped to her entity type)
- All receipt images and voice notes

### Retention and deletion

- Data is retained in **read-only mode for 90 days** to allow Alex to return
- After 90 days, she can request full deletion
- **Deletion certificate** issued (E39)

**No dark patterns. No retention games. Her data is hers.**

---

## 6. Never guess with no signal (D25, Principle 10)

**Hallucination-zero is a hard rule.**

- Penny makes a best-effort suggestion when she has at least one signal
- When she has genuinely no signal, she asks: *"I don't recognise this vendor — can you help me categorise it?"*
- A blank card with an honest question is **better** than a confident wrong answer

Applies to:

- Vendor identification
- Category inference
- Tax treatment
- Financial Q&A (Penny says "I don't have enough data to answer that accurately" rather than guessing)

---

## 7. Never ship streak mechanics (D62)

- No streaks
- No daily-usage targets
- No loss-aversion gamification

**Why this is a hard rule:** streaks work for language learning (downside = "my Spanish didn't improve"). In money, loaded with anxiety, streaks **accelerate avoidance**.

---

## Supporting principle — Alex owns her ledger (Principle 12)

Reinforces rule 5. On cancel, Alex takes her full ledger with her. Full portability is a hard rule, not a feature.
