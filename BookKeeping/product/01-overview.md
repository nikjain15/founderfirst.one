# 01 — Overview
*What Penny is · The problem · Who Alex is.*

---

## The one-paragraph product

**Penny** is an AI-first, mobile-first bookkeeper for US solopreneurs. The core interaction is conversation — not forms, not dashboards. Penny notices money moving in the world, categorises it intelligently, and presents it to the owner as a one-tap approval card. The owner confirms, or corrects, in seconds. Books stay clean without the owner ever needing to know what "double-entry bookkeeping" means. The growth engine is word-of-mouth: Penny wins by being the product Alex actually recommends.

---

## The problem

Solopreneurs — freelance designers, developers, consultants, coaches, writers — run profitable businesses on top of broken bookkeeping. The tools available (QuickBooks, Xero, FreshBooks, Wave) were built for accountants or for businesses with employees. The experience is: open the app, feel lost, close the app.

The result:

- **Tax surprise** every April
- **No sense of real income** — gross revenue feels good, take-home is a mystery
- **Unpaid invoice anxiety** — awkward to chase, so she doesn't
- **Receipt chaos** — expenses scattered across Gmail, Stripe, PayPal, bank feeds
- **Quarterly tax confusion** — either overpays or gets penalised
- **CPA bill is a mess tax** — $400–$800 for cleaning up the year she should have cleaned up herself

**The anxiety is not about earning enough. It is about not knowing how much she actually gets to keep.** Penny removes that unknown.

## Who Penny is for — Alex

MVP persona — full version at `../persona-freelancer.md`.

| | |
|---|---|
| **What Alex does** | Freelance designer, developer, consultant, writer — sells skills, not products |
| **Business structure** | Sole proprietor, single-member LLC, or S-Corp-elected (post-$80–100k net income) |
| **Years freelancing** | 1–6 years — past panic, not yet systematised |
| **Annual gross income** | $55,000 – $250,000 (launch range includes S-Corp band per D72) |
| **Location** | Any US city, any state; 100% US-only at launch |
| **Clients** | 3–8 active at any time; paid per project or on retainer |
| **Employees** | None; occasional subcontractor for overflow work |
| **Primary device** | iPhone — where financial life happens in real time |
| **Review device** | MacBook — when she has time to sit down |
| **Payment methods seen** | Stripe, PayPal, Venmo, Zelle, CashApp, ACH, check |
| **Current setup** | Bank app + Stripe dashboard + notes app + dread |
| **Tools tried and abandoned** | QuickBooks Online, Wave, spreadsheet that worked for 2 months |
| **What she responds to** | Simple, opinionated tools — Notion, Linear, Superhuman |

In her own words:

> *"I know I should be tracking this stuff. I just don't know where to start, and every time I try to figure it out I feel like I need an accounting degree. I just want someone to tell me if I am okay."*

---

## What success looks like for Alex

- She knows her real take-home number within 60 seconds of opening the app
- She never gets surprised by a tax bill again
- Her CPA bill goes down — she arrives with clean, organised books instead of a mess
- Tax season feels like clicking a button, not a crisis
- She recommends Penny to the next freelancer who complains about taxes or their CPA bill
