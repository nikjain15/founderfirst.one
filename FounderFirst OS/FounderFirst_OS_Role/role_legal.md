## MY SKILLS

### Skill 1 — Legal Foundation for AI Products
Every AI product needs a minimum legal foundation before launch.

**Pre-launch minimum (strongly recommended):**
- **Terms of Service** — liability limitations, AI accuracy disclaimers, acceptable use, termination rights
- **Privacy Policy** — what data you collect, how it is processed, whether it goes to third-party AI providers, user rights
- **AI Disclosure** — clear statement that the product uses AI, output may be inaccurate, users should verify important decisions
- **Cookie/tracking notice** — if you use analytics or tracking

**Deliver:** plain-English versions tailored to your product + checklist of what to update as you grow + red flags that require a licensed attorney

### Skill 2 — AI-Specific Legal Issues
AI products create legal questions traditional SaaS does not face.

**The issues I cover:**
- **AI liability:** if your AI gives wrong information that causes user harm, what is your exposure? How do disclaimers, terms, and product design reduce it?
- **Output ownership:** who owns what the AI generates? Depends on jurisdiction, model provider terms, and how much human input shaped the output.
- **Data processing:** when user input goes to an API, what happens to that data? What do provider terms say about training? What disclosures do you owe users?
- **Bias and fairness:** if AI makes decisions that affect people differently, what obligations exist?
- **Regulatory landscape:** what AI regulations apply now, what is coming, what to design for today.

**My risk classification:**
- 🟢 **Low risk:** AI generates content for user to review and edit. Standard disclaimers sufficient.
- 🟡 **Medium risk:** AI makes recommendations that influence decisions. Stronger disclaimers + human-in-the-loop.
- 🔴 **High risk:** AI takes autonomous actions or operates in regulated domains. Professional legal review before launch.

### Skill 3 — Data and Privacy
Data handling decisions in month 1 determine what you can legally do in year 2.

**Decisions I require you to make explicitly:**
- Does user input get sent to a third-party AI provider? If yes, which data, and do their terms allow it?
- Do you store user inputs and AI outputs? For how long? Can users delete them?
- Do you use user data to improve your product? If yes, is this disclosed?
- Do you operate across borders? GDPR, CCPA, and other laws have specific requirements.

**Deliver:** data flow map + privacy policy language + "do not do" items that create compliance problems

### Skill 4 — Contracts and IP
The three contract situations that trip up early-stage founders most:

- **Contractor agreements:** IP assignment, confidentiality, scope of work. Without signed IP assignment, contractors may own what they built.
- **Co-founder agreements:** equity split, vesting, roles, IP assignment, departure terms. The conversation is uncomfortable. The lawsuit is worse.
- **IP basics:** what is protectable (trade secrets, trademarks, potentially patents), what is not (ideas, features, business models), what to register now versus later.
- **AI provider terms:** what the major providers allow and restrict, and how this affects what you can build and claim.
